AgentSkills '26

The First Workshop on Agent Skills — Design, Evaluation, and Optimization of Procedural Knowledge for LLM Agents
May 26, 2026 · San Jose, CA · Full day · Submissions due Apr 12

Overview

LLM-based agents are rapidly moving from research demos to production systems—but their effectiveness hinges on the procedural knowledge they can access at inference time. How should that knowledge be structured, shared, and verified? Agent Skills are an emerging answer: structured packages of instructions, scripts, and references that augment agents without model modification.

In practice, a Skill is a SKILL.md file adopted across Claude Code, Gemini CLI, OpenClaw, Codex, and 30+ agent platforms. The research momentum is striking: eight focused Skills papers appeared in a single month (Feb–Mar 2026). An audit of 3,984 Skills found 37% contained security flaws. SkillsBench revealed that curated Skills improve pass rates by 16.2 points on average, yet self-generated Skills can hurt performance in nearly a third of tasks.

The field is moving fast and needs a dedicated venue. AgentSkills '26 brings together researchers and practitioners working on Skills design, benchmarking, optimization, security, and ecosystem infrastructure.

Topics of Interest

We invite contributions across the full lifecycle of Agent Skills, including but not limited to the topics below. This list is not exhaustive—we welcome submissions in any related area.

  1. 1.Design & Authoring — Principles for effective Skills; structure–efficacy relationships; domain-specific patterns
  2. 2.Evaluation & Benchmarking — Frameworks treating Skills as first-class artifacts; metrics beyond pass rate
  3. 3.Synthesis & Optimization — LLM-based generation; RL refinement; evolutionary approaches
  4. 4.Composition & Scaling — Multi-Skill effects; interference and synergy; optimal selection
  5. 5.Domain Applications — Healthcare, manufacturing, scientific computing, cybersecurity
  6. 6.Ecosystems & Infrastructure — Registries, versioning, distribution; interoperability
  7. 7.Security & Supply Chain — Malicious Skill detection; formal verification; supply-chain attacks
  8. 8.Continual Learning — Feedback-based improvement; trajectory mining for Skill discovery
