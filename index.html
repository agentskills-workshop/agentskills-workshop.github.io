Overview

Agent Skills are structured packages of procedural knowledge that augment LLM agents at inference time without model modification. A Skill is a SKILL.md file with instructions plus optional scripts and references—adopted across Claude Code, Gemini CLI, OpenClaw, Codex, and 30+ agent platforms.

Eight focused Skills papers appeared in a single month (Feb–Mar 2026). An audit of 3,984 Skills found 37% contained security flaws. SkillsBench revealed that curated Skills improve pass rates by 16.2 points on average, yet self-generated Skills can hurt performance in nearly a third of tasks.

The field is moving fast and needs a dedicated venue. AgentSkills '26 brings together researchers and practitioners working on Skills design, benchmarking, optimization, security, and ecosystem infrastructure.

Topics of Interest

We invite contributions across the full lifecycle of Agent Skills, including but not limited to:

  • 1
    Design & Authoring. Principles for effective Skills; structure–efficacy relationships; domain-specific patterns; failure mode analysis.
  • 2
    Evaluation & Benchmarking. Frameworks treating Skills as first-class artifacts; metrics beyond pass rate (cost, token efficiency, reliability).
  • 3
    Synthesis & Optimization. LLM-based generation (GEPA); RL refinement; evolutionary approaches; prompt optimization.
  • 4
    Composition & Scaling. Multi-Skill effects; interference and synergy; optimal selection and retrieval.
  • 5
    Domain Applications. Healthcare, manufacturing, scientific computing, cybersecurity, and other verticals.
  • 6
    Ecosystems & Infrastructure. Registries, versioning, distribution; interoperability across agent harnesses.
  • 7
    Security & Supply Chain. Malicious Skill detection; formal verification; sandboxing; supply-chain attacks and defenses.
  • 8
    Continual Learning. Feedback-based improvement; trajectory mining for Skill discovery; agent self-improvement.

Call for Papers

We accept two tracks:

  • Full papers — up to 6 pages + references
  • Short papers / demos — up to 4 pages + references

Submissions must follow the ACM SIGPLAN template and will be reviewed single-blind with at least two reviews. We encourage releasing Skills, prompts, evaluation harnesses, or other reusable artifacts alongside submissions.
